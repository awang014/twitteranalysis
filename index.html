User analysis of #ChatGPT based on Twitter data

Anyu Wang | 16/01/2023

ChatGPT

OpenAI launched an AI chatbot called ChatGPT in November 2022. Its comprehension and superb language organization skills are very powerful. Making up stories is an easy task for it. It can even write requests for resumes, write poetry, emails and code. It is considered a super dialogue model. Since it has be launched, it received a lot of buzz due to its powerful performance and application areas. To understand who is using and talking about it and how people are using and commenting on it, I conducted a user analysis with Twitter data.

Data Acquisition

Data is collected by TAGS, by searching Twitter and capturing all tweets (including tweets, retweets, replies and quotes) that contain the hashtag #chatgpt within a certain period of time. After 3 days of data collection, a total of 29,111 pieces of data were collected. In the dataset, the first data was collected on 26 December 2022 at 12:45 pm and the last one on 1 January 2023 at 9:21 pm. The duration is 6 days, 8 hours and 36 minutes. The average rate of tweets was 3.2 tweets per minute. Overall, #chatgpt is a topic that is being talked about a lot.

User Analysis

In this section, the characteristics of the users will be analysed. As you read this part, you can generate an initial profile of ChatGPT's users, including their authority, geographical and social attributes. As well I will provide you with links to the homepages of some of the top users, including experts and interesting amateur players. If you want to learn more about them, you could find that in this section.

Rweets or Retweets

As the pie chart on the right shows, the number of original tweets in the dataset is close to the number of retweeted tweets. The total number of retweets is 15,345, slightly more than the 13,765 original tweets, but they are still relatively equal. Clearly both are quite important.

However, are retweeters and tweeters the same group of users? Will a lot of retweeters be bots? Who are the top tweeters and retweeters? Are there some key opinion leaders? To what degree do they influence other users?The data is then filtered and analysed. Let's take a deeper look at the tweeters and retweeters to answer these mentioned questions.

In order to make the next analysis closer to the real users, the data with high suspicion of bots are removed and the dataset is given a cleanup.

User Locations

Perhaps you are wondering where the users are coming from. In this part you can get a general idea about the popularity of Chat GPT in different regions.

Surprisingly, none of the users added geographic coordinates when they posted. But we can get an idea of where users are coming from by the location information of the profiles. Among them, 65.6% of the users show the location.

Sorting the location information, we can see that the country with the most users is USA, followed by France, India, UK and Canada.

User behavior

In order to have a clearer picture of users' tweets, the figure below shows a visualization of the number of tweets they send. Most users send only 1 tweet, and the rest are concentrated in 2-5 tweets. There are not many users who tweeted or retweeted a lot in the dataset time, and they have a higher probability of being bots.

Demographic groupings

The users' friends on Twitter were analyzed to see if the users belonged to a group in some way. You can see as shown on the right that users with between 100 to 500 and between 1k to 5k friends make up the majority.

Attitude Analysis

Chat GPT is a very powerful bot that allows users to ask it to write a poem or calculate a maths problem. It can perform many different tasks. You might be interested in what people talk about under this topic, or curious if users are happy with Chat GPT. This section gives you some insight into the user attitudes.

Emotions

The top thirty tweets were ranked by the number of retweets and the number of replies. MonkeyLearn was used to perform sentiment analysis on the text content of these tweets, and the results showed that the text contained positive attitudes with a 92.3% confidence level. This means that users' attitudes towards Chat GPT are largely positive.

DanielSoper provides a tool to score the sentiment of the text on a scale of -100 to +100. -100 indicates a very negative or serious tone and +100 indicates a very positive or enthusiastic tone. DanielSoper scored the above text 98.7 on the sentiment scale. You can see that the tone of the user is very enthusiastic.

Conclusion

ChatGPT is hotly discussed on Twitter and popular among all kinds of users. This article analyzes the basic profile of users, including their geographic location and their social connections. Users' behaviors, such as the number and frequency of tweets and retweets, are analyzed. Also, users' attitudes, including emotions, concerns, etc., are discussed. From these three dimensions it shows who the users are, what they are doing and what they are thinking. In short, the user group is huge. Users are from the United States most. It also contains some top opinion leaders. Regular users, who make up the vast majority more, don't invest a very large amount of time in tweeting. There were six days in total, but the majority of people only sent one tweet or only one reply. Large part of the word frequency discussed by users is related to the daily use of Chat GPT. Art, video and e-books were the highest discussed application areas. The majority of the most popular posts were also tutorials on everyday usage with ChatGPT. Users rated it largely positive and showed enthusiasm. This can give information for the design and research about ChatGPT.
